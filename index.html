A Bit About Me

My Story

First, thanks for stopping by. This site's primary purpose is a Geographic Information System (GIS) Portfolio of sorts. My interests however tend to span a wide variety of topics so I will use this medium to cover those as well. These include, but are not limited to, family and travel, technology (finding the appropriate balance) and Geography (my profession). As you read along, I will try to provide updates on all and hopefully keep a blog updated.

In 2003, I had just graduated from the University of Georgia and was living in Jackson Hole, Wyoming. After my roomates decided to move to Boone, North Carolina to kayak I figured "what the heck" that sounds like a nice place. I knew from my time at UGA, Apppalachian State University had a strong Geography and Planning program so I started an email correspondence with a professor in the department. I slowly determined that a Geography and Planning Master's Degree may be what I wanted to pursue. I will profess at 23 I still really had no clue what I wanted to do with my "career". This whole Geography thing did however seem intereseting. So after landing a job at the Mellow Mushroom (my previous experience in Athens helped) I packed up my belongings and moved to Boone. I worked for a year to establish residency and bought a study guide at the local thrift store to prepare for the GRE. After starting my program, I was told I needed a challenge so was put into the Advanced GIS class. Mind you, I'd only ever heard of GIS in undergrad and never taken a real class. I spend my nights in the library and by the end of the first year had a full assistantship and was a teaching assistant for Cartography. The three years I spent in Boone allowed me to really determine where I wanted to grow professionally and personally. I met so many awesome people and really feel I'll be back in western North Carolina eventually.

After two years at App State, I decided to move back to my hometown of Pelham, Georgia and took at job at the Southwest Georgia Regional Commission. I enjoyed my job however was not fully immersed in GIS like I had envisioned. Thus, I decided to move to the Georgia coast and take a job with Glynn County GIS. After my first day, I knew this was the perfect place for me. A rich GIS department with great staff allowed me to pursue my interests and immerse myself in GIS. I was also able to make a lot of great friends whom I still keep in contact.

After a stint in central Georgia, working at Robins Air Force Base, I ended up back "home" in southwest Georgia. I currently work in Tallahasee, Florida as a GIS Administrator/Developer with exp Inc. I have a beautiful wife, Sara who works as a WIC nutritionist and we just had a baby boy Randall Emerson Chew III "Remy". I have done a lot in my life and been a lot of places, but simply hanging out with my family blows all that away. As I progress in my career and life, I strive to constantly learn and explore. It has been a great ride thus far and I look forward to what the future holds. Ok, that was bit corny.

Interests

As with a lot of GIS folk, I came out of school with a rich ESRI background. If anything, they have done a brilliant job marketing to the academic sector. I primarily use their platform today and have seen it grown from ArcView 3.x to the current "ESRI 3.0" as I heard it recently referred. Since that time I have been extremely fortunate to work with a host of platforms in and out of GIS. My interests are constantly changing so the items below may change a bit over time as I think they should.

Simply being a GIS practicioner is not enough in today's field. We must learn also learn the most appropriate methods in which to disseminate our data. This has taken the form of web and mobile delivery.

To that end, I have found it imperative (as well as extremely interesting) to learn lightweight, flexible methods to share information. The emergence of cloud delivery has opened a whole host of opportunities. The biggest being the chance to learn at a low cost.

Leaflet

This presentation pretty much sums up my thoughts about leaflet and "simple" maps. I've tried to convey the theme of a map simply with the data present--no fancy tools or tables/legends needed. Leaflet epitimizes this approach. Lightweight and clean, leaflet is something we should all strive to learn.

Amazon AWS

A gamechanger for sure, Amazon AWS has allowed for the rapid deployment of services at an extremely low cost. Scalable and secure, we must leverage the resource to better server our needs. This site is running of an ec2 Amazon Linux API.

QGIS

QGIS or Quantum GIS, is a powerful suite of Geographic Analysis tools ranging from desktop to QGIS Server. I have thoroughly enjoyed learning it and find it a great alternative to traditional propietary software. Using it and the lizmap client I was able to recreate a basic web mapping portal where maps are created in QGIS desktop and served out via QGIS Server/lizmap. The possibilities are endless with an open soure technology such as QGIS.

Workflow Automation

Efficiency when dealing with project workflows is critical. To that end, I have become adept at leveraging popular scripting languages such as python, Powershell etc to assist in this automation. More importantly, I know when and where to employ efficiencies and when a more human approach is needed.

Pelham Chamber of Commerce

The Pelham, Georgia Chamber of Commerce needed a site update and more control of their content. I was asked to deliver a solution in which I could build out their site and then turn over the keys for day to day management. Utilizing squarespace I was able to effectively deliver this solution.

Pecan Report

Bailey Farms was in need of a way in which to ingest daily Pecan Pricing from the USDA and display this information in a user friendly format. I used workflow automation combined with .net development to build out a dynamic web form. Utilizing Amazon AWS and PostgreSQL, I was able to build a site to meet Bailey Farms Needs.

Geospatial Analysis

Proficient with a wide array of geospatial tools, I can effictevely and efficiently provide geographic insight into complicated problems. I seek the simplest path forward and strive to provide just that from my results--a simple yet effective solution to a complicated geospatial problem. I also struggle with telling people "what it is I do".

LizMap Web Mapping Client

Out of sheer curiosity I wanted to build a complete web mapping portal using all open source tools. To accomplish this I used QGIS Server and the LizMap Web Client to deliver a complete online GIS Portal. PostGIS was used for data storage and QGIS used for data loading and analysis. The entire platform was cheap, effective and efficient.

This Web Site

This web site runs off the Amazon Linux AMI. I also have a PostGIS database I use to store any GIS Project I may be working on at the time. It is a micro instance but serves my purpose wonderfully. Running git on the server allows me to quickly update the site with ease.
